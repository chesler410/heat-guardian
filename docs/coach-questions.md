# 🏊 Questions for a local swim coach

A conversation guide to validate Heat Guardian and learn how meets *really* work. Lead with
curiosity (let them talk), not a feature pitch. ~20–30 min over coffee. Pick the ones that fit.

## 1. How a meet actually runs (set the scene)
- Walk me through a typical meet weekend from the team's side — what happens before, during, after?
- Who runs the timing/scoring system at your meets? Is it always Hy-Tek Meet Manager, or something else?
- How do swimmers and parents find out heat & lane assignments on the day?
- What usually goes wrong / what's the most chaotic part of meet day?

## 2. How results & data flow (the heart of it)
- Once a race finishes, how do the results get *out* — to the scoreboard, to parents, online?
- Do your meets publish **real-time results to the web**, or only Meet Mobile, or PDFs afterward?
- Do families pay for / rely on **Meet Mobile**? What do they like and dislike about it?
- Where do you and parents go to see official times after a meet (SwimCloud, USA Swimming, the host site)?
- Is there *any* point where results are public/shareable that an app could read automatically?

## 3. What parents struggle with
- What do parents ask you (or each other) most often during a meet?
- How do parents track "is my kid up soon, what lane, did they make their cut?" today?
- For families with **more than one swimmer**, how do they keep track of everyone?
- What do new/first-season parents find most confusing?

## 4. Does Heat Guardian actually help? (show it, then ask)
- *(Show the arm table + cuts.)* Would this be useful on the deck? What's missing or wrong?
- The "next time cut to beat" per event — is that how swimmers/parents think about goals?
- "Find a meet near you" — where would you expect that data to come from? Would you trust it?
- **Share-by-code** (one parent imports the heat sheet, the team pulls it) — would your team use that?

## 5. The swimmer-facing idea (be candid)
- Some kids manage their own meet (older swimmers, or a parent who can't attend). Useful, or no?
- We're exploring **AI "how'd my meet go?" feedback** from a swimmer's own notes + times —
  encouraging, never harsh. As a coach, does that *help* or does it step on your toes?
- Any concerns about kids using a phone app at meets, or about their data?

## 6. Adoption & trust
- Would you ever recommend an app like this to your team's families? What would it take?
- What would make you *distrust* or avoid a tool like this?
- Is there an existing tool your team already relies on that this would have to coexist with?

## 7. Open the floor
- If you could wave a wand and fix one thing about meet-day tech for families, what would it be?
- Who else should I talk to — a meet director, a timing volunteer, a parent rep?

---
*Tip: ask permission to take notes, and end by asking if you can follow up. The §2 answers about
how results flow are the most strategically valuable — that's the data we're trying to reach.*
